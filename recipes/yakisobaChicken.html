Yakisoba Chicken

Description

The dish typically starts with thinly sliced chicken breast or thigh meat, which is marinated in a flavorful mixture of soy sauce, mirin (sweet rice wine), and sometimes a touch of ginger and garlic for added depth of flavor. The chicken is then stir-fried until cooked through and slightly caramelized on the edges, imparting a rich and savory taste.

Ingredients

  • breast or thigh meat, thinly sliced
  • Yakisoba noodles (pre-cooked)
  • Vegetables such as cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and onions, thinly sliced or julienned
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ginger, grated (optional)
  • Sugar or honey (optional, for sweetness)
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish (optional)
  • Green onions, finely chopped, for garnish (optional)
  • Vegetable oil, for stir-frying

Steps

  1. Slice the chicken thinly, prepare the vegetables, and loosen the yakisoba noodles if using fresh ones.
  2. Combine the sliced chicken with soy sauce, mirin, minced garlic, and grated ginger (optional) in a bowl. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until cooked through and lightly browned. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add more vegetable oil if needed. Add the sliced vegetables and stir-fry until tender yet still slightly crisp.
  5. Add the pre-cooked yakisoba noodles to the skillet with the vegetables. Stir-fry until heated through and well combined.
  6. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and oyster sauce. Optionally, add sugar or honey for sweetness.
  7. Add the cooked chicken back into the skillet with the noodles and vegetables. Pour the sauce over the mixture and toss until evenly coated.
  8. Transfer the yakisoba chicken to serving plates or bowls. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!
